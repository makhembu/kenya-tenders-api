# Phase 6 — Tournament Round 3: 3 → 2 (Kill the Pretender)

## The Single Load-Bearing Assumption

### 1. SaccoSync (Umbrella Ledger)
- **Load-bearing assumption:** Umbrella organizations (federated Chamas/Societies) have the organizational authority to mandate sub-group data syncing.
- **Attack 1 — What if wrong?** If sub-groups refuse to sync data out of privacy or autonomy concerns, SaccoSync becomes a reporting tool with zero data, and umbrella admins won't pay for empty reports.
- **Attack 2 — Evidence:** Phase 2 research found regional Burial Societies (e.g., Mol Mat Group) with clear hierarchical structures and centralized funeral benefit funds. This suggests a central authority already exists.
- **Attack 3 — Comparable failure:** Many federated Sacco ERPs in East Africa have failed because sub-Saccos didn't want the "central body" seeing their bad loans.
- **Verdict:** **SURVIVES** (The hierarchical nature of Burial Societies documented in Phase 2 provides strong evidence of central authority).

### 2. BakiPay (Dividend Automation)
- **Load-bearing assumption:** Treasurers prioritize "automation accuracy" over their personal manual control (Excel).
- **Attack 1 — What if wrong?** If Treasurers fear losing the "opaque" nature of manual Excel (which allows for small "discretionary" adjustments), they will actively block a transparent, rule-based API.
- **Attack 2 — Evidence:** Chamasoft's success (cited in Phase 1) proves that 20,000+ groups in Kenya are already willing to move to digital-first transparency.
- **Attack 3 — Comparable failure:** None found in the logic-layer niche; most failures were in the "full app/wallet" space.
- **Verdict:** **SURVIVES** (The problem is purely a logic one, not a trust one, and documented digital adoption in this niche is high).

### 3. StandingWatch (Sports Logic)
- **Load-bearing assumption:** Real-time data accuracy in lower leagues is worth more to fans/admins than the "free but slower" Facebook updates.
- **Attack 1 — What if wrong?** If fans are satisfied with checking a Facebook photo of a handwritten table at 9 PM, then the branch secretary won't pay KES 1,000/month for a "live" API.
- **Attack 2 — Evidence:** Evidence from FKF branch circulars shows a high volume of disputes and appeals over "manual PDF errors." This proves accuracy *is* a pain point.
- **Attack 3 — Comparable failure:** "ScoreAF" (hypothetical/generic local score sites) often fail because they lack the "official" branch seal of approval.
- **Verdict:** **ELIMINATED** (The load-bearing assumption is hard to verify within 30 days of launch; fans may simply not care enough about API-driven widgets to force branch secretaries to pay).

1. **BakiPay** (The Logic Layer for Dividends)
2. **SaccoSync** (The Admin Layer for Umbrella Orgs)

*StandingWatch is eliminated as its value depends on fan engagement, which is more volatile than the administrative pain solved by the other two.*
